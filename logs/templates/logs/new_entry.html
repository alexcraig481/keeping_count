Your Rights

Before reading the questions and starting your entry, please remember and repeat the following:

  • I have the right to my own needs and feelings and to have them be as important as anyone's else's
  • I have the right to experience my feelings and to express them, if I want to.
  • I have the right to not be held responsible for other people's feelings.
  • I have the right to express my opinions.
  • I have the right to say no.
  • I have the right to set limits and boundaries.
  • I have the right to be listened to respectfully.
  • I have the right to choose how to live my life.
  • I have the right to decide what my priorities are.
  • I have the right to be independent if I want to.
  • I have the right to decide how I spend my time
  • I have the right to change myself, my behaviors, my values, my life situation, and my life.

- Taken from "Boundaries after a Pathological Relationship" by Adelyn Birch

Question Prompts

  1. Do you feel you have no voice in the relationship, like you are unimportant?
  2. Are you regularly accused of overreacting, being over-dramatic or too sensitive?
  3. Do you feel like a failure as a partner even though you work hard to please your partner or "get it right"?
  4. Does you partner constantly blame or criticize you?
  5. Does it feel forbidden to express negative thoughts and emotions?
  6. Does your partner seem impossible to please? Is there constant complaining about some aspect of your personality, your looks, or the way you choose to live your life?
  7. Does you partner need to be control all or most aspects of your life?
  8. Does you partner routinely ridicule, dismiss, or disregard your opinion, thoughts, suggestions, and feelings?
  9. Does it feel like you are expected to live by a separate set of rules that your partner created?
  10. Do you find yourself apologizing a lot?
  11. Does your partner tease you, make fun of you, or use sarcasm as a way to put you down or degrade you? Does your partner claim it was a joke or an attempt to help you?
  12. Do you find yourself making excuses for your partner's behavior?
  13. Do you feel sure your partner's intent is not malicious and therefore any hurtful words or behavior are a misinterpretation on your part?

- From or Inspired by "Escaping Emotional Abuse" by Beverly Engel
